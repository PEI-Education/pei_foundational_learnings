English Language Arts

~[if#chkGrade.~([Students]grade_level)=8] [else#chkGrade] [/if#chkGrade]
Reading Comprehension Learners can respond literally, inferentially and evaluatively to a variety of texts.
Literal Learners can (by writing, representing or speaking):
  • Find the answers to most literal questions using explicit information found in the text.
  • Identify the important details in order to summarize the text.
~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_8_READ_LIT)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_8_READ_LIT) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
Inferential Learners can (by writing, representing or speaking):
  • Use background/prior knowledge and hints found in the text to make judgements.
  • Find examples in the text to support the judgements made.
~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_8_READ_INF)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_8_READ_INF) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
Evaluative Learners can (by writing, representing or speaking):
  • Make text-to-self connections; make text-to-text connections; make text-to-world connections.
  • Explain how the personal connections they make help them to understand the text.
  • Explain how the author's use of craft influences the reader's point of view.
  • Suggest ideas from a different perspective.
  • Evaluate purpose, structure and characteristics of a variety of texts and explain how they contribute to the understanding of the text.
~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_8_READ_EVA)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_8_READ_EVA) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
Writing Traits Learners can create written multi-paragraphed texts using three of the writing traits: ideas, organization, and conventions.
Ideas Learners can, through writing:
  • Present a specific topic with main ideas.
  • Consider purpose and audience.
  • Include relevant details that support the topic.
  • Incorporate useful information from resources (when applicable).
~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_8_WRIT_IDE)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_8_WRIT_IDE) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
Organization Learners can, through writing:
  • Produce a specific structure that matches topic, purpose and audience (see text forms on page 52 of the Curriculum Guide).
  • Express related ideas in paragraphs using a topic sentence, supporting details and a closing sentence.
  • Use transition words between paragraphs to show evidence of logical sequencing.
~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_8_WRIT_ORG)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_8_WRIT_ORG) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
Conventions Learners can, through writing:
  • Use correct end punctuation and capitalization.
  • Apply correct grammatical structures that make the text readable (e.g. subject/verb agreement, verb tense, numbers, apostrophes, contractions, plurals, conjunctions, punctuating of dialogue/quotes, and parts of speech).
~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_8_WRIT_CON)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_8_WRIT_CON) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
