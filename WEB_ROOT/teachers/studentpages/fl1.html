Mathematics

~[if#chkGrade.~([Students]grade_level)=1] [else#chkGrade] [/if#chkGrade]
1 N1 Say the number sequence, 0 to 100, by: 1s forward and backward between any two given numbers; 2s to 20, forward starting at 0; and 5s and 10s to 100, forward starting at 0. ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_N1)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_N1) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 N2 Recognize, at a glance, and name familiar arrangements of 1 to 10 objects or dots. ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_N2)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_N2) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 N3 Demonstrate an understanding of counting by: indicating that the last number said identifies “how many”; showing that any set has only one count; using the counting on strategy; and •using parts or equal groups to count sets. ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_N3)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_N3) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 N4 Represent and describe numbers to 20 concretely, pictorially and symbolically. ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_N4)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_N4) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 N5 Compare sets containing up to 20 elements to solve problems using: referents; and one-to-one correspondence. ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_N5)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_N5) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 N9 Demonstrate an understanding of addition of numbers with answers to 20 and their corresponding subtraction facts, concretely, pictorially and symbolically by: using familiar and mathematical language to describe additive and subtractive actions from their experience; creating and solving problems in context that involve addition and subtraction; and modelling addition and subtraction using a variety of concrete and visual representations, and recording the process symbolically. ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_N9)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_N9) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 N10 Describe and use mental mathematics strategies (memorization not intended), such as: counting on and counting back; making 10; doubles; and using addition to subtract to determine the basic addition facts to 18 and related subtraction facts. ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_N10)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_N10) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 PR3-RR3 Describe equality as a balance and inequality as an imbalance, concretely and pictorially (0 to 20). ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_PR3_RR3)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_PR3_RR3) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 SS1-FE1 Demonstrate an understanding of measurement as a process of comparing by: identifying attributes that can be compared; ordering objects; making statements of comparison; filling, covering or matching. ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_SS1_FE1)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]Math_1_SS1_FE1) [else#Y] [/if#Y]

English Language Arts

~[if#chkGrade.~([Students]grade_level)=1] [else#chkGrade] [/if#chkGrade]
1 SL3.1 Demonstrate a growing awareness of social conventions such as turn-taking and politeness in conversation and co-operative play ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_SL31)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_SL31) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 RS1 Monitor reading and self-correct when reading does not make sense, sound right and/or look right; reread to check for meaning (may require occasional prompting) ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_RS1)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_RS1) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 RS2 Use meaning, word structure (plurals, roots and ing, ed, s endings), language structure (e.g., predictable word order and language patterns), and phonics (e.g., initial/final/medial sounds; onsets and rimes; common spelling patterns) to solve simple words; may require occasional prompting ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_RS2)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_RS2) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 RS3 Recognize common high frequency words and personally significant words ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_RS3)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_RS3) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 LC1 Respond accurately to most literal questions by identifying specific facts; rely on information located in one place in the text, rather than skimming complete text ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_LC1)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_LC1) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 WS5 Use sound/symbol knowledge, word structures (ed, s, ing, endings), familiar onset and rimes and word chunks to write unknown words ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_WS5)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_WS5) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 WTI1 Choose a general topic and often support or extend written ideas with drawings ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_WTI1)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_WTI1) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 WTS1 Use simple sentences, many of which are complete; may rely on repetitive patterns ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_WTS1)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_WTS1) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
1 WTC4 Use conventional spacing between words ~[if#Y.~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_WTC4)$"Y"] ~([Students.U_PEI_FL]ELA_1_WTC4) [else#Y] [/if#Y]
